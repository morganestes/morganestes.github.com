Morgan W. Estes

I build websites.

I’m a web developer living in the Oklahoma City metro area. I mostly work in PHP, HTML, CSS, jQuery, and MySQL, but am always open to learning and using new tools. Lately I’ve been working on expanding into CoffeeScript, Node.js, and responsive design.

I’m a big fan of WordPress, the semantic web, typography, and the fun that HTML5 brings to the table (but not table layouts).

I dabble in digital photography, and even had one of my pictures published in the Oklahoma Bar Journal.

I've designed and developed a few websites. Here's a sample:

  • OBA Annual Meeting 2010

    Built this website on the WordPress CMS with a customized design based on the Louise Brooks theme.

  • OBA Annual Meeting 2011

    Built on WordPress Multisite and a customized version of the Spectacular theme.

  • OBA Tech Fair

    One of my earliest micro-sites, I designed this steampunk-themed site to promote the first-ever technology fair hosted by the Oklahoma Bar Association.

  • Law Day

    This is the most recent version of a website to promote this annual event.

    Built on a custom PHP framework and enhanced with jQuery, each year has a slightly different theme that iterates on the design from the previous year.

  • Women In Law Conference (2010)

    I worked with a graphic designer on this site, and converted his PSD to a pixel-perfect matched website.

    I built a custom PHP framework for this site that was reused internally for several other websites. This was one of my first sites to use CSS3 to achieve the look that matched the static design.

  • OBA Solo & Small Firm Conference

    This is the most recent version of a website to promote this annual conference.

    Built on a custom PHP framework and enhanced with jQuery, each year has a slightly different theme that iterates on the design from the previous year.

  • Lawyers Helping Lawyers Foundation, Inc.

    I built this site to promote a fundraiser for the LHL Foundation.

    This reponsive site is built on WordPress, and the design is from a PSD template that I converted to a WordPress theme. It contains a custom login form, and a special admin area to create dynamic text for the donation graphic.

  • Oklahoma Lawyers for America’s Heroes

    Launched in November 2010, this site is home to one of the largest programs to assist Oklahoma veterans and service members with legal issues.

    The site is custom-built with elements of the PHP-based Kirby Toolkit and MySQL on the back end, and HTML, CSS, and jQuery on the front.

  • Unpacking the Boxes

    This website was a quick build for a class for ladies new to the OKC/Edmond area.

    The site was built on the Weebly platform, and uses a Wufoo form for registration.
